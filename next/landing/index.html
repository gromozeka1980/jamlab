Relational instruments

You can’t play a wrong note here.

JamBrew is a pocket instrument where keys are steps along a scale. Whatever you press is in tune. Just play.

Try it right here — the real koto from the app (hirajōshi scale):

Tap the keys — every press is in tune. ▶ brings in the band. Drag a key upwards — that’s a bend. Slide a finger along the dots — a glissando. This is one instrument of nine, locked to one scale, with no video recording — the rest lives in the app.

How it works

Steps, not notes

A piano has 12 notes per octave and most of them are wrong for your solo. Here a key means “one step up” or “three steps down” — along the scale of the style you chose. There is nowhere to miss.

Nine worlds

Blues with real bends, a koto in the rain, desert trance of the East, the bronze of a gamelan, synthwave neon… Each style brings its own scale, its own voice and its own backing band.

Record and show off

One button turns your improvisation into a video with a live visualizer — ready for any messenger or feed.

A note from the developer

I build JamBrew myself, and I’m building it for the long run. This is not a weekend experiment — it’s an instrument I want to keep alive and growing for years.

Bug reports get fixed fast — usually in the next update. Feature requests are read, every one of them: half of the best ideas in the app came from conversations with the first players.
